Newyork City

The Empire State Building is one of New York's most famous landmark buildings
and key tourist attractions. The 381-meter-tall, 102-storey building was the
tallest in the world until the 1 World Trade Center tower rose higher, 41 years later.
Topped with a mooring mast for airships,the Empire State Building immediately
became a landmark and a symbol for NYC when it opened in 1931.





Paris

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art,
fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by
wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower
and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral,the city is known for its cafe
culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré




Tokyo

Tokyo, Japan’s busy capital, mixes the ultramodern and the traditional,
from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The opulent Meiji Shinto Shrine
is known for its towering gate and surrounding woods. The Imperial Palace sits
amid large public gardens. The city's many museums offer exhibits ranging from
classical art (in the Tokyo National Museum) to a reconstructed kabuki theater
(in the Edo-Tokyo Museum)
